Associate Director of Data Analytics and Strategy

The Associate Director of Data Analytics and Strategy will collaborate with various external stakeholders to drive the long term data strategy for enterprise data integration while creating a systematic approach to data governance, stewardship and change management. In this role, you will be a key member of a team whose mission is to enable socially responsible, efficient inter-agency data sharing by providing strategic guidance on data and technology processes. This role has a high degree of visibility so the suitable candidate should be comfortable planning, creating and delivering persuasive presentations


  Responsibilities

  • Manage and grow the Worker Connect initiative by actively engaging agency leadership and users, developing new use cases and promoting system capabilities.
  • Identify new digital and data opportunities, users and data sources for inclusion large-scale data integration efforts.
  • Define and implement management processes for maintaining data governance policies and standards within a complex policy and legal framework.
  • Oversee the analysis and design of analytic or business intelligence initiatives; ensure appropriate requirements are delivered to technical teams.
  • Utilize data governance framework, technology capabilities, processes/procedures and implementation plans to drive data standards across the enterprise.
  • Write clear document deliverables that will be shared with legal counsel, program staff and executive leadership.
  • Ensure integrity and quality of product deliverables through ongoing monitoring, testing and metadata management of data sources, products and initiatives.
  • Manage policy and business resource team.
  • Promote the goals of social service data integration pursuant to City’s OneNYC Plan.
  • Track developments in data technologies while identifying new external trends and data innovations.
  • Ensure that our technology roadmap and investments are aligned with the business data strategy.


  Skills We're Seeking

Commitment to the mission of advancing greater equality and opportunity.

Solid exposure to any or all of the following: business intelligence, data integration, data governance, data analytics or science, system interoperability, master data management.

Entrepreneurial spirit – ability to find creative solutions to difficult problems and a willingness to pitch-in and help out to accomplish our project’s strategic goals.

Strong business analysis skills.

In depth knowledge of HHS Agencies and programs.

Public speaking, clear and concise writing, highly professional demeanor with excellent communication and presentation skills, both verbal and written

Ability to self-direct work.

Client-focused, committed to rapid execution, and strategic and flexible.

Proficient in MS Office: PowerPoint and Excel.


  Education and Experience

At least five years of data management experience, preferably in City government and/or for HHS-related entities. Three – five years of experience supervising staff, either directly or as part of a project team.

1. A master's degree from an accredited college in economics, finance, accounting business or public administration, human resources management, management science, operations research, organizational behavior, industrial psychology, statistics, personnel administration, labor relations, psychology, sociology, human resources development, political science, urban studies or a closely related field, and two years of satisfactory full-time professional experience in one or a combination of the following: working with the budget of a large public or private concern in budget administration, accounting, economic or financial administration, or fiscal or economic research; in management or methods analysis, operations research, organizational research or program evaluation; in personnel or public administration, recruitment, position classification, personnel relations, employee benefits, staff development, employment program planning/administration, labor market research, economic planning, social services program planning/evaluation, or fiscal management; or in a related area. 18 months of this experience must have been in an executive, managerial, administrative or supervisory capacity. Supervision must have included supervising staff performing professional work in the areas described above; or

2. A baccalaureate degree from an accredited college and four years of professional experience in the areas described in "1" above, including the 18 months of executive, managerial, administrative or supervisory experience, as described in “1” above.

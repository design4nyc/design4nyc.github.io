Design Researcher — Contract

The Design Researcher will use their expertise to research and design public services to decrease poverty and increase opportunity for New Yorkers. This individual has solid experience developing and executing human-centered research plans, conducting fieldwork, capturing and synthesizing information from various stakeholders, and analyzing and developing key insights that can lead to actionable solutions and measurable outcomes.

This position will sit within the Service Design Studio at the Mayor’s Office of Economic Opportunity (NYC Opportunity). The ideal candidate is comfortable with responding to rapid requests in a fast-moving environment, and should carry a strong understanding of designing for policy and government. This role will work closely with the design, data, technology and programs and evaluations teams at NYC Opportunity

This is a 2-4 month full-time position, focused on a specific project.

  Responsibilities

  • Research existing procedures and practices to understand pain points and barriers to services.
  • Orchestrate user research and testing to understand and capture the experiences and perspectives of relevant stakeholders and experts both inside and outside government (ex. clients, staff, administrators, etc.), including planning, outreach and facilitation.
  • Turn user research insights into well-communicated actionable items and deliverables, including journey maps, research and insight reports, enhancement lists, etc.
  • Develop wireframes, mockups and useful and informational visualizations, final design documentation, and style guides for new and existing services.
  • Contribute to the overall development of strategy, design, deliverables, and final products.
  • Provide recommendations on how design approaches can be applied across other relevant key service areas.

  Education and Experience

Bachelor’s degree in design preferred with 2-4 years of experience in design research, service design and digital services development. Experience in policy, government or non-profit, in particular with low-income and vulnerable youth and families is a plus. Please also indicate if you speak other languages and indicate level of fluency.

Note that all applicants must be authorized to work the United States. We are unable to provide sponsorship.

  Skills We're Seeking

Commitment to the mission of advancing greater equality and opportunity through human-centered design practices in government.

Design in any/all of the following practices: user experience, human-centered design, interaction design, user research, data visualization, and service design.

Development of research-based deliverables, including journey maps, research reports, enhancement lists, etc.

Facilitation of workshops with diverse stakeholders.

Experience with visual design tools such as Adobe Creative Suite.

Strong collaboration with design, product, technology, business and performance and evaluation team members.

Experience with an agile, iterative development process.

A working fluency in Spanish is preferred but not required.

Public sector experience is preferred but not required.

  Rate and Hours

The Design Researcher will be paid at an hourly rate of $27.50. The position will be 35 hours a week for approximately 2-4 months.

NYC Opportunity Collaborative Design VISTA Fellow

The application window for NYC Opportunity's VISTA Fellowships are now open.

The Mayor’s Office for Economic Opportunity (NYC Opportunity), uses evidence and innovation to reduce poverty and increase equity. We advance research, data, and design in the City’s program and policy development, service delivery, and budget decisions. Our work includes analyzing existing anti-poverty approaches, collaboratively developing new interventions that address unmet needs with City agencies, facilitating data sharing across City agencies, and rigorously assessing the impact of key initiatives.

NYC Opportunity released a Call for Collaborative Innovations to New York City government agencies, seeking proposals for how targeted funding can promote collaborations to improve outcomes for vulnerable residents. The proposed initiatives aim to strengthen partnerships and overcome silos within, allowing City agencies and nonprofits to address poverty-related challenges more holistically.

The Collaborative Design for Antipoverty Programs will expand NYC Opportunity’s capacity to advance its mission of designing, implementing, and evaluating effective anti-poverty programs that lead to improved economic opportunity for disadvantaged populations.


  Responsibilities

As part of the Programs and Evaluation Team, the NYC Opportunity Collaborative Design VISTA Fellow will be primarily responsible for working with agency partners and NYC Opportunity staff to:

  • Identify performance metrics and create implementation work plans.
  • Develop community outreach plans and engage community partners.
  • Develop online program resource banks with common materials.
  • Develop, verify, and document functional and technical specifications.
  • Build capacity for project management of new and existing programs.
  • Make recommendations to high-level decision makers on the allocation of resources based on program performance.
  • Other duties as assigned.

  Skills We're Seeking

Commitment to the mission of advancing greater equality and opportunity.

Strong analytical skills.

Experience with project management, policy analysis, and research.

Ability to present ideas cleraly and concisely in writing and orally.

Client focused, committed to rapid execution, and strategic and flexible.

Highly organized and detail‐oriented.

  Education and Experience

Undergraduate degree or higher. Prior office experience and public service experience strongly preferred.

Policy Fellow, Data Strategy and Policy

The application window for the Mayor’s Office for Economic Opportunity Policy Fellowship is now open.

Within NYC Opportunity, the Enterprise Data Solutions (EDS) team supports meaningful data integration that allows City agencies to increase efficiency and access to benefits and services. To do this work, we use proven strategies to responsibly bring together data. In addition, the EDS team develops technology and applications that allow for independent rapid on-demand matching capabilities that operate within strict legal frameworks.

For successful data integration some key components must be present. The methodology employed by the EDS team includes knowledge gathering, user research, and data governance. This helps us pull together data in an intelligent and cohesive manner, and allows us to better understand the data and the consumer.

As part of the EDS team, the Policy Fellow will have an opportunity to assist in using digital services and public policy to create meaningful impact and help improve the lives of millions of New Yorkers. Fellows must be able to commit to working for a minimum of 6 months, full or part time, with a minimum of three days a week.

The Fellowship is paid by the hour and will begin in early to mid-January.


  Responsibilities

  • Research and pursue strategies to raise awareness about EDS products and services. This could include developing strategies for marketing to stakeholders and participating in sector relevant events and competitions.
  • Plan convenings (e.g., meetings, panels, conferences, etc.) of users of EDS data products and services and other interested parties.
  • Write document deliverables to be shared with stakeholders, the public, legal counsel, program staff and executive leadership.
  • Develop and manage social media content for EDS, including identifying photo opportunities that effectively illustrate EDS’s work.
  • Perform other duties as assigned.

  Skills We're Seeking

Commitment to the mission of advancing greater equality and opportunity.

Strong analytical skills.

Ability to self-direct work.

Client focused, committed to rapid execution, and strategic and flexible.

Proficient in MS Office: PowerPoint and Excel.

Highly organized and detail‐oriented.

  Education and Experience

Fellowships are open to undergraduate, graduate, and recent graduates in economics, information technology, data science, finance, sociology, political science, urban studies or a closely related field.

Applicants must be authorized to work in the United States. We are unable to provide sponsorship to apprentices.

  How to Apply

Please submit your coverletter and resume.

Successful candidates will be invited for an initial phone screen. Due to the volume of applications received we may not be able to respond to every application.

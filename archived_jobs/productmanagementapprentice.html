Product Management Apprentice

The application window for Summer Apprenticeships is now closed. Please check back in July for more information about our Fall Apprenticeships.

A product management apprenticeship with our team is an opportunity to grow your expertise in product development and management, design, digital services, or public policy and help improve the lives of millions of New Yorkers. We are currently seeking students and recent graduates with an interest in these disciplines: product management and development, public policy, or social impact.

Apprenticeships are paid by the hour, part-time or full-time, with a minimum of three days per week. Summer 2018 Apprenticeships will run from the beginning of June until the end of August. Projects will focus on the delivery, on-going success, and continuous improvement of government services.

  Responsibilities

Based on your skills and interests, responsibilities will include:

  • Contribute to product strategy and success metrics, including Key Performance Indicator (KPI) models, monthly analytics, roadmaps, etc.
  • Assist with vendor and stakeholder management.
  • Assist in sprint management—working across teams to assure sprints are completed on time and to specification.
  • Participate in the development and execution quality assurance (QA) testing of products.
  • Assist in defining feature and functionality requirements.
  • Participate in user research and synthesis.
  • Assist in the creation of prototypes, design assets, and project documentation.
  • Collaborate in the creation of product development and management documents, including roadmaps, project briefs, and user stories.

  Education and Experience

Apprenticeships are open to undergraduate, graduate, and other product and design training program students and recent graduates.

  Skills We're Seeking

Commitment to the mission of advancing greater equality and opportunity through human-centered design practices in government.

Previous experience in product management or development, or managing the design and development of technology projects.

Ability to develop metrics and produce reports for products and initiatives.

Turning user research insights and data into new product features or enhancements.

Technical and design fluency and organization to support product vision and development.

Interest in using plain language to help residents access and interact with government services.

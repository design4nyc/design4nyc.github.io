Communications Design Apprentice

The application window for the Mayor’s Office for Economic Opportunity Apprenticeships is now closed

An Apprenticeship with the NYC Mayor’s Office for Economic Opportunity is an opportunity to grow your communications strategy, design and production expertise. You will be working with a small, impactful team creating public services to improve the lives of millions of New Yorkers. We are currently seeking students with an interest in graphic design, communications and social media strategy to help us amplify our work and support in streamlining our communications process across the wider team.

Apprentices are paid by the hour, full or part time, with a minimum of three days a week beginning on September 10, 2018 through December 19, 2018.

  Responsibilities

Based on your skills and interests, responsibilities will include:

  • Contribute to a communications strategy and framework to be utilized across teams at NYC Opportunity.
  • Assist the Design Director with evolving the Service Design Studio brand.
  • Develop content for newsletters, social media, external presentations and long-form stories.
  • Surface opportunities for press coverage and build contact lists for NYC Opportunity projects.
  • Lead the production of visual assets for various communications channels.
  • Research opportunities for events, conferences and speaking engagements.

  Education and Experience

Apprenticeships are open to undergraduate or masters’ students or recent graduates with a background in communications and/ or design strategy.

Applicants must be authorized to work in the United States. We are unable to provide sponsorship to apprentices.

  Skills We're Seeking

Commitment to the mission of advancing greater equality and opportunity through human-centered design practices in government.

Interest in using plain language to help residents access and interact with government services.

Excellent written and verbal communication skills.

Proficiency in Adobe Creative Suite and Microsoft Office.

Familiarity with in any/all of the following practices: user experience, human-centered design, interaction design, user research, data visualization, and service design.

Strong project management skills, and comfort working independently in a fast-paced environment.

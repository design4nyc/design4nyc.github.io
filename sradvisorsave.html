Senior Advisor

The Senior Advisor will work with City agency partners to support and coordinate the design, implementation, program management, evaluation for multiple initiatives and projects. They will lead and manage a portfolio of programs, including the Save for College expansion, as well as a portfolio of new and existing initiatives, special projects, and evaluations across a range of issue areas affecting low-income New Yorkers, generated by NYC Opportunity and YMI.

One key project that this position will work on relates to the NYC Kids RISE Save for College Program. Launched in 2018, the initiative is a scholarship and savings program designed to make college and career training more accessible for all NYC public school students— regardless of family income or immigration status. In partnership with NYC Kids RISE, every kindergarten student enrolled in a participating NYC public school automatically receives a NYC Scholarship Account with an initial $100 allocation on their behalf, unless their parents/guardians “opt out” of the program. The City will invest $15 million annually.

Additional programmatic areas of focus may include asset building, workforce development, education programs, health/behavioral health programs, out of school youth, and others.


  Responsibilities

Responsibilities include:

  • Conduct strategic planning and oversight of the Save for College initiative, working with City partners at the NYC DOE and NYC Department of Consumer and Worker Protection.
    • Conduct stakeholder consultation and coordination, landscape assessment, evidence-based policy research, and service design.
    • Provide oversight of program operations and performance management strategy.
    • Provide fiscal management (i.e,, contracting, invoice reviews).
    • Support execution of research and learning agenda.
  • Work with City agencies to develop and implement new programs, and to manage a portfolio of existing initiatives.
    • Develop and oversee work plans, budgets, data collection protocols, human centered design practices, and performance management strategies, in alignment with existing practices and systems.
    • Provide ongoing feedback to City agency partners on performance data and fiscal management.
  • Manage the design and execution of evaluations of NYC Opportunity and other City initiatives.
    • In partnership with project stakeholders and external evaluators, lead evaluation scoping, development of solicitation and evaluation vendor selection, and evaluation vendor contract management.
    • Coordinate report finalization and the dissemination of evaluation findings to various constituencies. Translate technical evaluation information to policymakers and other stakeholders.
  • Review, reflect, and analyze our work in program operations, evaluations, data, research, and related areas,
    • Generate insights, summary reports, and written products, and share insights via meetings, speaking engagements, developing talking points, etc.
    • Engage City agency partners and external audiences including policymakers through meetings, conference presentations, and helping to organize events.
  • Conduct research to support new service design, service refinement, and policy development.
    • Respond to rapid research requests into program and service areas, research-based practices to assist low-income populations, and other Mayoral priorities.
    • Research best practices and translate research into actionable information for policymakers and service providers.
    • Develop funding proposals for new initiatives, as needed.
  • Supervise staff, fellows, and/or interns, as well as tasks assigned to others, as needed.
  • Execute special projects, as necessary.

  Skills We're Seeking

Commitment to the mission of advancing greater equality and opportunity.

Experience working closely with communities who are engaging with public services and programs and/or has lived-experience engaging with public services and programs.

Experience in policy, government or non-profit, in particular with low-income and vulnerable youth and families is a plus.

Ability to think creatively and proactively, embrace new approaches, and pioneer innovative solutions to intricate problems, while working collaboratively with a diverse constituency.

Experience with project and performance management, program evaluation, and knowledge of policies/strategies to support low-income populations. Ability to become familiarized with new issue areas and relevant research bases as needed.

Interest in leading a project through design, execution, and completion while engaging the support of the team/management as appropriate while balancing multiple projects. Ability to problem solve, and balance competing priorities while paying close attention to detail and meeting deadlines.

Familiarity with analytic and evaluative concepts to analyze and recommend policies and procedures. This includes but is not limited to knowledge of evaluation methodologies, and ability to analyze complex systems and propose innovative approaches to multi-faceted problems facing low-income New Yorkers. Experience managing evaluation consultants a plus.

Familiarity with contract, budget, or fiscal management, serving in oversight role and collaborating with partners to meet deadlines.

Strong interpersonal skills and judgement; ability to establish productive and collaborative relationships with all levels of staff.

A motivated individual who can contribute positively to a learning culture and collaborative team and is willing to perform a range of tasks in a dynamic and rapidly responsive organization. Effective and creative leadership ability.

Familiarity with asset development, economic mobility, and education a plus.

Strong oral and written communication skills with an attention to detail.

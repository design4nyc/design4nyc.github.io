Communications Designer (Temporary Position)

We are seeking someone who is passionate about designing and crafting information to uplift the work of the Service Design Studio and the larger NYC Opportunity team. This is a role meant to support various initiatives and internal communications across our team, with a focus on:

  • Storytelling projects visually and through the written word
  • Translating complex or abstract concepts into easy to understand visuals and diagrams
  • Developing social media, branding assets, and document templates to promote our Studio and our larger office’s work

This is a temporary position for up to 8 months. This role is hosted by the Mayor's Fund to Advance New York City, a 501(c)(3) non profit organization that works with NYC Opportunity and the NYC Service Design Studio. While the role is hosted by the Mayor's Fund, the day to day work will be within the NYC Service Design Studio at NYC Opportunity. This role is not on a city staff line.


  Responsibilities

Ideally, a strong candidate for this role would have a strong foundation in communication, branding, and visual design; carry a diverse toolkit of multidisciplinary design skills that can be applied across different topics; and have experience writing materials such as process documentation, blogs, and recommendation reports.

While your primary focus will be to support and advance the work of the Service Design Studio, you will also be working across NYC Opportunity, supporting overarching office-wide and team-specific communication and design needs. A successful candidate is one who is mission-driven, cares about improving public services, comfortable navigating ambiguity and complexity, proactive about learning and applying new skills and thinking to the work, and who enjoys participating in a cross-collaborative environment. This role reports to various managers across the Studio team and will depend on the project.

Responsibilities include:

  • Visually translating complex or abstract concepts into understandable maps, diagrams, or wireframes (eg. journey maps, service blueprints, stakeholder maps, information architectures, infographics, and other visual explainers).
  • Supporting the preparation of workshop and meeting facilitation materials such as presentation decks, digital white board templates (eg. Miro), and digital and analog worksheets, etc. to run successful stakeholder meetings.
  • Designing outreach and communication materials for Studio initiatives (eg. social media, recommendation report layouts, final project deliverables, etc.)
  • Stewarding and maintaining the standards of our Service Design Studio brand, NYC Opportunity brand, and project identities.
  • Contributing time and skill in promoting, storytelling and bringing overall clarity to the work of the Studio and the larger office. This includes, but is not limited to, composing blog posts, and supporting how we visualize and verbalize formal communication materials for Studio initiatives (eg. insight and recommendation reports).
  • Participating in service design activities that help inform your design work and professional growth. These activities include, but are not limited to, supporting information gathering and community feedback and ideation sessions; reviewing, transcribing, and synthesizing research materials; and providing technical assistance for in-person and virtual workshops.

  • Participating in Studio capacity-building activities (eg. Office Hours, workshops, Civic Design Forums, Tools + Tactics). These activities include, but are not limited to, note-taking, data entry, content design and writing, planning support, and facilitation support.
  • Participating in internal Studio team and officewide planning, team-building, and culture-building activities; and promoting and supporting a culture of care and thoughtfulness.
  • Performing duties as assigned by the Director of Design and project leads, and support fellow team members as needed.

  Skills We're Seeking

We understand that candidates may not have all of the skills we are seeking below. We encourage those interested to apply even if they do not meet all of them. We are looking for talented individuals to join our team.

Commitment to the mission of advancing greater equality and opportunity.

Experience working closely with communities who are engaging with public services and programs and/or has lived-experience engaging with public services and programs.

Experience in policy, government or non-profit, in particular with low-income and vulnerable youth and families is a plus.

Proficiency in a variety of digital software design and prototyping tools such as Adobe CS, Figma, Miro, Google tools, etc.

Understands and has employed participatory research and community-centered design research and engagement practices.

Strong analytical skills and ability to understand and synthesize complex systems.

Strong communication writing and content design skills.

Ability to handle sensitive or confidential information in a caring and professional manner.

Experience in user experience (UX) research and development, and user interface (UI) design and development is a plus.

  Education and Experience

A baccalaureate degree from an accredited college and four years of professional experience in social design, anthropology, public administration, public policy, economics, information technology, data science, finance, sociology, political science, urban studies or a closely related field, and two years of satisfactory full-time professional experience in government, or non-profit or social impact sector.

  Benefits and Compensation

This is a temporary, full-time position. The Mayor’s Fund provides its employees (temporary positions included) with a robust benefits package.

The salary range for this position is $52,000 - $57,000 over 8 months commensurate with experience and qualifications.

  How to Apply

Please attach your resume and cover letter in an email to fund@cityhall.nyc.gov with “Communications Designer” in the subject line.

Due to the volume of applications received we may not be able to respond to every application.

This position will require work from our office in Downtown Brooklyn. All new staff are required by the City of New York to be vaccinated against Covid-19.
